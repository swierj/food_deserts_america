Methodology

The Data

  • Low Access: For rural areas, it accounts for people that are 10 miles or greater away from any grocery store. For urban area, it accounts for people that are 1 mile or greater away from any grocery store.
  • Low Income: People that are considered low income by the United States

In order to have usable data for the project, our group had to gather data from multiple datasets. One dataset did not have all the data we needed, so we used various methods to combine and merge our data together such as using Google Colab to use python and the pandas library to merge together data sets and export the data. We also used excel to filter and extract the necessary data, and used csv to json converters to format the data so that it can be usable.

The Maps

Our maps use Open Street Map to display the results for our project. For our first and second map, using data from the UDSA we are able to map areas that are either low access or low access and low income to a grocery store based on a certain distance away depending on if the area is in a rural or urban area. The third map is a porprotional symbols map of the median household incomes of each county in the US.
